Thinking is important: it’s how we make sense of the world and of new information. Thinking is also social: we build our ideas through our interactions with others. At thinkinwords we want to show students how to think about thinking and how to think with other people: a systematic approach to more effectively analyse and engage with the world around them.

By learning how to interrogate ideas — not just those of others, but their own as well — students can identify and probe the reasons that substantiate positions. Reasons should be valid and credible, but can be dubious and unjustified, and students can learn to categorically distinguish between these. Our programme sees these abilities of intellectual exploration and evaluation as an essential part of education.

We aim to put the focus back on the methodology — the systematic processes — of thought. Over our programme we want to create a safe and immersive thinking space that is distinct from the regular school environment. Here we will collaboratively examine content from a variety of disciplines and from the students’ own lives to create a community that thinks methodically, reflexively, and analytically.
